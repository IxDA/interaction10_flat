It’s interesting to consider how depictions of the future say quite a lot more about the present. Perhaps films took over these depictions from spectacles like The World’s Fair? The 1933 Chicago World’s fair had some facinating images. Their deterministic motto makes me wonder whether they were really optimistic or resigned: “Science Finds, Industry Applies, Man Conforms.” The New York 1939 World’s fair with its “world of tomorrow” theme explicitly set out to imagine the future (just when things seemed particularly bleak for the world). So much of the 30′s and 40′s seemed dedicated to imagining future domestic technologies. Does it seem like the futures we imagine now are all interfaces for public spaces and work?
